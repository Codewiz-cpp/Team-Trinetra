Mission & Innovation

Team Trinetra & Mayoor: Engineering a Lifeline

What started as a focused team of two has evolved into a powerhouse of seven members and five mentors driven by a singular mission: saving lives. At the heart of our operations is Mayoor, our custom-built uncrewed aerial system engineered for high-stakes environments. Mayoor is designed to deliver critical autonomous relief drops such as essential water bottles, emergency beacons” providing immediate, precise aid to human survivors in post-disaster scenarios.

SUAS 2026: Technology in Action for Recovery and Relief

This season, Team Trinetra is tackling the SUAS 2026 "Storm Response" challenge, exploring the vital role robotics play in community resilience and disaster recovery. Our systems are built to turn chaos into coordinated action across three critical phases: conducting rapid damage assessment to map environmental hazards, searching debris fields to deploy supplies, and collecting public safety data to support first responders. We aren't just restoring what was lost; we are leveraging technology with purpose to rebuild smarter.

TEAM TRINETRA

About Us

Our Mission & Vision

Representing the Madhav Institute of Technology & Science under the banner of the Dronex Aerotech club, Team Trinetra UAV is a multidisciplinary student engineering team preparing for the international SUAS 2026 competition. The team's mission is to leverage autonomous systems to advance disaster recovery frameworks, specifically focusing on aerial area inspection, hazard monitoring, and critical rescue logistics.

Team Lead

Parth Soni

As our Team Lead, Parth balances two key responsibilities: providing leadership and organization to keep the team on track while also overseeing the end-to-end electrical architecture of the UAV platform, including PCB layout, power distribution board design, and multi-rail voltage regulation for flight-critical subsystems.

His leadership is reflected in his structured approach—coordinating cross-domain payload mechanism robotics and flight-time optimisation strategies, balancing power budgets against mission endurance requirements.

Artificial Intelligence

Ankit Gurjar

Autonomous Navigation & AIML

Develops autonomous waypoint path-planning algorithms and integrates ArduCopter mission scripting for fully autonomous sortie execution including payload release sequences.

Fuses LIDAR point-cloud data with IMU and GPS streams for precise obstacle-aware navigation, enabling safe low-altitude flight in cluttered environments.

Serves as the primary test pilot, executing hardware-in-the-loop simulation runs before transitioning algorithms to live flight trials on the competition platform.

Moksh Dandotiya

Computer Vision & AI Systems

Trains and deploys YOLOv8-based object detection pipelines for real-time aerial target identification, optimised for latency-constrained onboard inference on edge hardware.

Implements OpenCV-based image processing routines for target geolocation, coordinate projection, and mission-event triggering integrated with the autopilot command layer.

Manages ground station visual display overlays, providing operators with annotated live feeds, confidence scores, and bounding-box telemetry during autonomous search missions.

Electrical

Harsh Vardhan Kaushal

PDB Design & Communications

Designs multi-layer PCBs for flight controllers, sensor buses, and custom carrier boards, ensuring signal integrity and EMI compliance in vibration-intensive airframes.

Responsible for power distribution board architecture, including eFuse protection, current sensing, and regulated output rails for avionics and payloads.

Integrates camera modules and RF communication subsystems into the electrical stack, managing antenna placement and coaxial routing for maximum link margin.

Rishabh Dohare

Ground Station & Telemetry

Operates the ground control station during field missions, monitoring MAVLink telemetry streams and managing uplink commands to the autopilot in real time.

Designs and validates failsafe logic — including RTL triggers, geofence enforcement, and battery-critical land sequences — to ensure safe autonomous behaviour under edge-case conditions.

Maintains and tunes the RF telemetry link, configuring data rates, channel hopping, and RSSI thresholds to sustain reliable communication at extended operational ranges.

Mayoor?

How we got the name

The name "Mayoor"—referencing the peacock—is a deliberate design ethos for our platform, signaling a shift toward precision, structural ingenuity, and elegance in aerial robotics. By prioritizing aerodynamic efficiency and intelligent autonomy, the system achieves mission objectives through refined flight kinematics rather than brute force. We have engineered the platform to minimize power draw while maximizing endurance, ensuring that its operational capabilities are as resilient as they are graceful. Ultimately, this architecture transforms the UAV from a mere tool into a high-performance system capable of sophisticated, autonomous navigation.
